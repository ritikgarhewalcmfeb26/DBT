1) Which field of the Customers table is the primary key?

Ans) The primary key is CNum (Customer Number). This field uniquely identifies each customer and cannot contain null values.


2) What is the 4th column of the Customers table?

Ans) The 4th column is Rating. (The standard structure of this specific table is typically Cnum, Cname, City, Rating, Snum).

3) What is another word for row? For column?

Ans) Row: Tuple, Record

Column: Attribute, Field

4) Why isn’t it possible to see the first five rows of a table?

Ans) In a Relational Database Management System (RDBMS), tables are mathematically defined as unordered sets of data. This means rows are not stored or retrieved in any guaranteed sequential order. Because there is no inherent concept of "first" or "last" at the storage level, you cannot reliably ask a database for the "first five rows" unless you apply an explicit sorting rule (like an ORDER BY clause in SQL) to define what "first" means.
